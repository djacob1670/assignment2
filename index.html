The crisis within a crisis: How food insecurity in New York exploded the age of COVID-19

By Denny Jacob

Nov. 14, 2020

The ripple effects of the coronavirus pandemic in the United States has resulted in more than lost lives. The issue of food insecurity also ballooned as more people became unemployed and dealt with greater financial uncertainty.

While Nassau County is only projected to see around 60,000 more food insecure people, this represents an 83% change from the total number of food insecure people in 2018.

While unemployment is only one of many factors behind food insecurity, it is especially notable as people lost access to a main source of income and likely filed for unemployment, if they even qualified, or relied on other avenues of relief like the one-time $1,200 stimulus check issued in April.

Queens County led the five counties in all three categories.

As some in the U.S. resist efforts to contain the virus, COVID-19 has flourished as a result. With President-elect Joe Biden set to take office in January, he has vowed to contain the pandemic. But if and when COVID-19 is subdued, multiple other crises like the issue of food insecurity will require his attention as well.
